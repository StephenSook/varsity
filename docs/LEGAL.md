# Legal, IP & deployment considerations

VARSITY is a research and accessibility prototype. This document is how the project handles
intellectual property, data licensing, and the path from a hackathon submission to a real
deployment. It is written for transparency, and because thinking past the demo is part of building
something that could actually reach blind fans. **It is not legal advice;** a real product should be
reviewed by qualified sports-IP counsel.

## Non-affiliation

VARSITY is an independent open-source project. It is **not** affiliated with, sponsored by, or
endorsed by FIFA, The IFAB, any football association, any league, any club, any broadcaster, or any
player. References to the 2026 World Cup are descriptive only. FIFA, World Cup, and the FIFA World
Cup Trophy design are trademarks of FIFA; VARSITY uses none of those marks, logos, or the trophy
image, and implies no official connection.

## Unofficial explanations

VARSITY **explains** VAR and offside decisions; it does **not adjudicate** them. Every explanation
is generated by an AI model from approximate freeze-frame coordinates and a rule-grounded prompt,
and may contain errors or approximations. The only authoritative ruling is the one made by the match
officials on the pitch. Do not rely on VARSITY for officiating, betting, broadcast, or any other
decision-making purpose. For a knife-edge call, VARSITY is built to say so and defer to the official
decision rather than quote a false-precise margin (the calibration critic, `services/app/verification.py`).

## Data sources and attribution

- **StatsBomb Open Data**: VARSITY's canned freeze-frames draw on data released by StatsBomb at
  https://github.com/statsbomb/open-data under the StatsBomb Public Data User Agreement (free for
  non-commercial research and educational use, attribution required). VARSITY consumes the data and
  produces a new derivative (an offside explanation); it does not redistribute the raw event data.
- **Sportmonks** and **API-Football** are referenced as pluggable live-trigger sources. The
  submission does not call live paid APIs on its critical path; the canned StatsBomb path plus a
  replay buffer are the deterministic floor. A production deployment would require a paid
  subscription and separate clearance of any league/club/player logos and photos, which those feeds
  do not grant.
- **IFAB Laws of the Game**: VARSITY grounds its narration in the IFAB Laws, primarily Law 11
  (Offside). The Laws are copyrighted by The IFAB. The narration paraphrases the rules in plain
  language; short verbatim clause citations are attributed to IFAB and used under fair-use
  principles for an educational and accessibility purpose. The full Laws are at https://theifab.com.
  **Honest note on the repository:** for offline mode and deterministic retrieval, the project
  commits a parsed, chunked copy of the Law text (`services/app/rag/index/`,
  `apps/web/public/ifab-index.json`). This serves the non-commercial educational/accessibility
  retrieval the doctrine is designed to protect, with IFAB attributed. A production deployment would
  move to fetching the Laws at runtime and seek a non-commercial educational cooperation with IFAB
  rather than ship the text statically.

## Player names, club names, and trademarks

Where commentary names a player, club, or competition (for example, "the attacker was in an offside
position"), the use is factual and nominative: it describes who did what in a real event. This is
consistent with nominative fair use (*New Kids on the Block v. News America Publishing*, 9th Cir.
1992) and the news-reporting exemption to trademark dilution (15 U.S.C. section 1125(c)(3)(B)).
VARSITY does **not** use club crests, league marks, FIFA marks, or player likenesses beyond textual
reference, and it implies no endorsement. The repository contains no club crests, league logos,
FIFA marks, the trophy image, or player photos.

## Models and open-source dependencies

Every model VARSITY ships (IBM Granite, Granite Guardian, Granite Nano, Kokoro, Transformers.js) is
Apache-2.0, and the runtime dependency tree carries no copyleft. The full per-component list is in
`THIRD_PARTY_LICENSES.md` and `NOTICE`. VARSITY itself is Apache-2.0 (`LICENSE`). IBM provides
uncapped IP indemnification on IBM-developed Granite models, which is relevant when scoping a future
commercial deployment.

## Privacy

VARSITY's primary mode is **on-device**: Granite Nano via Transformers.js and Kokoro run in the
user's browser. The screen-reader interaction, the audio output, and the prompt content do not leave
the device. There are no user accounts, no analytics, and no personal-data collection by VARSITY
itself. The only outbound calls are to the optional live-data trigger and to load model weights.
Under GDPR and CCPA, VARSITY is designed to minimize personal-data processing to zero.

## Accessibility

VARSITY is screen-reader-native by design, following WCAG 2.2 AA principles with the screen reader
as the primary interaction modality rather than an afterthought (see `docs/ACCESSIBILITY-SR-LANG.md`
for the assistive-technology compatibility matrix). Accessibility is the mission, not a checkbox.

## What a real deployment would take

A real consumer deployment during a live tournament would require:

1. **Licensed live VAR-decision data**, through a partnership with the tournament's official data
   rights holder or via a broadcaster that already holds the rights. The in-stadium football-data
   market is locked down by exclusive contracts (the *Football Dataco v. Sportradar* line
   established an EU database right; Genius Sports holds the exclusive in-stadia data partnership
   with Football DataCo through 2029), so this is a partnership, not a scrape.
2. **Co-distribution through an accessibility organisation** (RNIB, NFB, AFB, or similar) so VARSITY
   operates inside existing accessibility media licenses rather than as an unlicensed third party.
3. **A non-commercial educational cooperation with The IFAB** to deepen and formalise rule citations.
4. **Clearance of logos, player photos, and marks** even with a licensed data feed. VARSITY's
   audio-first design helps: the production UI stays text and audio led, with no club crests or
   player photos.
5. A formal Terms of Service, a third-party accessibility audit, and partnership-led go-to-market.

VARSITY is built to **partner into** existing rights and accessibility programs, not to route around
them. The submission demonstrates the technology stack and the screen-reader UX; the path to
production is partnership-first.

## Risk summary

| Area | Hackathon submission | Real deployment |
|---|---|---|
| StatsBomb data | Low (non-commercial + attribution) | High (paid license) |
| Sportmonks / API-Football | Low (not on the critical path) | Moderate (commercial license; logos/photos extra) |
| Live in-stadium / VAR-decision rights | n/a | High (Football DataCo / Genius / Sportradar rights regime) |
| Player names (factual) | Low (nominative fair use) | Low |
| Club logos / crests | Low (not in the repo) | Moderate (separate clearance) |
| FIFA marks / "World Cup" | Low (descriptive + disclaimer) | Moderate-to-high (ambush enforcement) |
| IFAB Laws text | Low-to-moderate (paraphrase + short quote, attributed) | Moderate (seek IFAB cooperation) |
| AI accuracy / hallucination | Low (disclaimers + calibration critic) | Moderate (needs a real ToS + monitoring) |
| Model + dependency licenses | Low (Apache/MIT/BSD; no runtime copyleft) | Low |
| Privacy / GDPR / CCPA | Low (on-device) | Low |
